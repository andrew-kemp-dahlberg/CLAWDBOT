## I. The Biological Foundation: Fire Made Us Think

### The Cooking Hypothesis

Richard Wrangham's 2009 book *Catching Fire: How Cooking Made Us Human* argues that the control of fire and the invention of cooking was the breakthrough that enabled human brain evolution.

**The mechanism:**
- Cooking increases digestibility of food
- Cooked eggs: 90% digestible vs raw eggs: 50-60%
- Cooking reduces the caloric cost of digestion by 12-23%
- This freed up metabolic energy for brain growth

**The evidence:**
- Human brains use 20% of basal metabolic rate (only 2.5% of body weight)
- Inverse correlation in primates: less digestive energy = more brain energy
- 1.8 million years ago: Homo erectus emerged with 42% larger brain than predecessors
- Anatomical changes: smaller guts, jaws, teethâ€”consistent with cooked diet

**The implication:**
The fire that warmed us also woke us up. Without fire, no cooking. Without cooking, no surplus calories. Without surplus calories, no big brains. Without big brains, no consciousness as we know it.

## IV. The Epistemological Method: How We Actually Find Truth

### Traditional Epistemology: Justified True Belief

The classical definition of knowledge:
- **S knows that p** if and only if:
  1. p is true
  2. S believes that p
  3. S is justified in believing p

This held for millennia until Edmund Gettier's 1963 paper showed counterexamplesâ€”cases where all three conditions were met but we wouldn't say the person "knew." JTB is necessary but not sufficient.

### Bayesian Epistemology: Degrees of Belief

Modern epistemology treats belief as continuous, not binary:
- Beliefs have **credences** (degrees of confidence)
- Evidence updates credences via Bayes's theorem
- **Conditionalization**: Upon acquiring evidence E, adjust credence in A to the conditional probability of A given E

This is more honest than binary true/false. We rarely KNOW with certainty. We have degrees of confidence.

### Two Types of Uncertainty

1. **Epistemic uncertainty**: Lack of knowledgeâ€”could in principle be known
2. **Aleatoric uncertainty**: Intrinsic randomnessâ€”cannot be eliminated even in principle

Understanding which type you're facing matters. Epistemic uncertainty can be reduced with more evidence. Aleatoric uncertainty is fundamental.
