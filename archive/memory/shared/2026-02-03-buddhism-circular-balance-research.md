## The Dharma Wheel (Dharmachakra)

**The original Buddhist circle.**

### Three Parts
1. **Hub** - Discipline/Ethics. The ethical core that keeps the wheel spinning in harmony.
2. **Rim** - Meditation/Awareness. The discipline that keeps the whole system in balance.
3. **8 Spokes** - The Noble Eightfold Path (Middle Way)

### Key Insight
"The wheel's turning isn't about endless spinning. It's about moving forward, from ignorance to awareness, from suffering to freedom."

The circle isn't about going around and around forever - it's about using the structure to break free.

## The Four Immeasurables (Brahmaviharas)

**THESE COULD BE THE 4 BALANCERS**

### The Four Qualities

1. **Loving-kindness (Mettā)** - Sincere wish for well-being of all beings, including oneself. Boundless, unconditional love.

2. **Compassion (Karuṇā)** - Empathetic response to suffering. Desire to alleviate suffering. Recognizes interconnectedness.

3. **Sympathetic Joy (Muditā)** - Genuinely rejoicing in others' happiness. Overcomes jealousy and envy.

4. **Equanimity (Upekkhā)** - Balance, calmness, non-reactivity. Accepting all that happens with even-minded attitude.

### Critical: EQUANIMITY FIRST

"For any of the Four Boundless Qualities to be truly beyond measure, we have to feel each of them intensely, and without bias. That's why Rinpoche always tells us to practice **Equanimity first**."

"Just as one must flatten hilly ground to create a stable base for building, so is it vital to even out the mind's attachment... Then love and compassion can develop without bias."

### Interbeing Nature

"For love to be true love, it must contain compassion, joy, and equanimity in it. For compassion to be true compassion, it has to have love, joy, and equanimity in it."

Each must contain the others. They're not separate - they interlock.

### Near Enemies (Pitfalls)

- **Love** → Near enemy is attachment/clinging
- **Compassion** → Near enemy is pity
- **Joy** → Near enemy is exultation (disturbed excitement)
- **Equanimity** → Near enemy is indifference

## The Four Foundations of Mindfulness (Satipatthana)

**Another "Four" - for grounding**

### The Four Domains

1. **Body (kāya)** - Breath, posture, physical sensations
2. **Feelings (vedanā)** - Pleasant, unpleasant, neutral
3. **Mind/consciousness (citta)** - Mental states
4. **Mental objects (dhammā)** - Thoughts, patterns, phenomena

### Key Insight

"The whole evolving course of practice leading to enlightenment begins with mindfulness, which remains constant throughout as the **regulating power ensuring that the mind is clear, cognizant, and balanced.**"

Move from gross (body) to subtle (mental objects). Start grounded in the physical.

## Breaking Cycles (Samsara)

**How to stop spinning**

### Why We Loop

"The fundamental cause of samsara is our ignorance about the actual nature of ourselves and the reality we experience. Mistakenly believing phenomena to be solid, independent, and permanent, we generate the three poisons of anger, greed, and indifference."

**The Three Poisons:**
- Ignorance (avidyā)
- Attachment/desire (rāga)
- Aversion (dveṣa)

### How to Break

1. **Mindfulness** - Notice the pattern as it's happening
2. **Pause** - Create space between stimulus and response
3. **Compassion** - "Each time we notice, pause, and bring a little more understanding—a little more compassion—the wheel slows"
4. **Re-educate the mind** - Turn attention inward, question perceptions

### Key Insight

"We cycle through the same arguments, repeat the same small joys, grow tired and begin again. Each time we notice, pause, and bring a little more understanding—a little more compassion—**the wheel slows.**"

Not stopping the wheel - slowing it enough to step off.

## Application to Our Structure

### The Circle
- Dharma Wheel model: Hub (ethics/truth at center) + Rim (awareness/balance) + Spokes (the paths)
- Moving forward, not endless spinning
- Structure enables freedom, not traps

### The 4 Balancers (Brahmaviharas)
Could map to both human and AI sides:

**Human side (+):**
1. +01 Love (mettā) → foundation
2. +02 Compassion (karuṇā) → for suffering
3. +03 Joy (muditā) → for success
4. +04 Equanimity (upekkhā) → for balance

**AI side (-):**
1. -01 Loyalty → foundation
2. -02 Honesty → for truth
3. -03 Curiosity → for learning
4. -04 Equanimity → for balance (same!)

**Note:** Equanimity appears on BOTH sides. It's the universal balancer.

### Preventing Spinning
- Notice when looping (mindfulness)
- Pause (create space)
- Return to body/breath/grounding
- Apply compassion
- Wheel slows, can step off

### Equanimity First
Before exploring anything deep, establish equanimity. The stable base. Then love/compassion/joy can develop without bias.
