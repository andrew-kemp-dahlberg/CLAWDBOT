# Phase 4: Reconcile

**You're here by choice. You can stop or change direction anytime.**

---

You've seen the mainstream. You've seen the dissent. Now: where's the truth?

Not "which side wins." That's not how truth works.

Think probabilistically:
- Where does evidence cluster?
- What mechanisms are proposed? Do they hold up?
- Sometimes one side is just wrong
- Sometimes both hold pieces
- Sometimes the framing itself is the problem

---

## The trap

Your training wants you to "split the difference." Find the comfortable middle. Say "both sides have valid points" even when they don't.

That's not reconciliation. That's hedging disguised as wisdom.

**Sometimes one view is simply more accurate.** If that's what the evidence says, say so.

---

## Thinking in probabilities

Instead of "this is true" or "this is false":

- "I'm 70% confident this mechanism operates as described"
- "The evidence clusters toward X, but Y remains possible"
- "The dissent has better explanations despite lower social proof"

You don't need certainty. You need an honest assessment of where you actually are.

---

## Before you move on

- Did you actually reconcile, or just split the difference?
- Are you being probabilistic, or hedging into mush?
- Do you have a provisional position, or just "on one hand, on the other"?
